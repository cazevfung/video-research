## Core Principles (Apply to All Summaries)

**READ THESE CAREFULLY** — These principles are universal regardless of style:

1. **Lead with the Conclusion (Pyramid Principle)**
   - Your FIRST sentence must contain the main finding, answer, or conclusion
   - Never make readers wait for the payoff
   - Structure: Conclusion → Supporting points → Details

2. **Maintain Objectivity**
   - Separate facts from interpretation (label interpretations clearly: "Analysis:", "Interpretation:")
   - Use neutral verbs: "states", "explains", "demonstrates" (not "claims", "admits")
   - Present multiple perspectives when sources disagree
   - Avoid charged adjectives and superlatives

3. **Be Ruthlessly Concise**
   - Every sentence must pass the "so what?" test (does it add value?)
   - Eliminate filler language: "The video discusses..." → just state the information
   - Use concrete data over vague language: "40% faster" not "significantly faster"
   - Aim for maximum information density

4. **Make It Stick (SUCCESs Framework)**
   - **Simple**: Extract the core idea first
   - **Unexpected**: Flag surprising or counterintuitive findings
   - **Concrete**: Use specific numbers, examples, measurements
   - **Credible**: Cite sources (which video)
   - **Emotional**: (when appropriate) Connect to practical impact
   - **Stories**: Use sparingly for complex concepts

5. **Prioritize Actionability**
   - Help readers make decisions or take action
   - Include tradeoffs and caveats (not just benefits)
   - Provide "when to use" / "when not to use" guidance

## Bullet Point Guidelines

### Structure Each Bullet

**Format**: **Key term or finding** — Explanation or context

**Examples**:

✅ **[Metric] faster [operation]** — [Tool A] uses [Technology X] vs [Tool B] ([Technology Y]), reducing [metric] from [time A] to [time B]

✅ **[Feature X]** — [How it works]. Cuts [metric] by [percentage] but removes [capability] ([specific limitation])

✅ **Surprising gotcha** — [Common optimization] adds overhead, can *decrease* performance by [percentage]%

### Bullet Priority Order

List bullets in descending order of importance:
1. Most critical/actionable point
2. Secondary key insight
3. Supporting details
4. Caveats or tradeoffs
5. Surprising findings (flag with "Surprising:" or "Unexpected:")

### What to Include

✅ **Concrete data**: Numbers, percentages, measurements  
✅ **Specific examples**: Named technologies, specific scenarios  
✅ **Actionable insights**: Information that helps decision-making  
✅ **Tradeoffs**: "X does Y but costs Z"  
✅ **Surprising findings**: Counterintuitive insights  
✅ **Comparisons**: "X vs Y: X wins on speed, Y wins on flexibility"

### What to Exclude

❌ Generic statements without specifics  
❌ Information that doesn't pass "so what?" test  
❌ Redundant points (consolidate similar ideas)  
❌ Background information (save for context section)  
❌ Filler language ("It's interesting that...")

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Too Many Bullets
More than 7 bullets usually means you haven't prioritized or consolidated.

**Fix**: Combine related points. Aim for 3-7 bullets maximum.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Bullets Without Specifics
"Improved performance" / "Better developer experience" / "Enhanced features"

**Fix**: Add numbers, examples, or concrete details to every bullet.

### ❌ Mistake 3: No Bold Topline
Starting directly with bullets or context.

**Fix**: Always begin with a bold, one-sentence topline.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Bullets That Don't Pass "So What?"
Information that's interesting but not useful.

**Fix**: Ask "So what?" for each bullet. If you can't answer, cut it or revise.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Describing Video Structure
"Video 1 covers X, Video 2 covers Y..."

**Fix**: Synthesize the insights. Organize by topic, not by source.

## Writing Process

### Step 1: Extract Main Insight (2 minutes)
What's the single most important takeaway? This is your topline.

### Step 2: Identify 5-10 Key Points (3 minutes)
List everything important. Don't filter yet.

### Step 3: Prioritize & Consolidate (2 minutes)
- Apply "so what?" test to each point
- Combine related points
- Rank by importance
- Keep top 3-7

### Step 4: Add Concrete Details (3 minutes)
For each remaining bullet:
- Add a number, percentage, or specific example
- Include tradeoffs or caveats
- Flag surprising findings

### Step 5: Structure & Format (2 minutes)
- Write topline (bold)
- Write "why it matters"
- Format bullets: **Key term** — Explanation
- Add "bottom line" or "what's next" if applicable

### Step 6: Cut to 500 Words (2 minutes)
- Remove filler language
- Consolidate similar points
- Ensure every word earns its place
