**Structure & Format:**
- Each research question becomes its own H2 section with a clear, descriptive title (use the question text as the section heading, or a clear paraphrase)
- Use H3 subsections to organize complex answers within each question
- Front-load concrete facts and key points in opening sentences of each section
- Answer questions in the order they are provided
- Include a final H2 "Conclusion" section after all questions have been answered

**Title Writing Rules:**
- **Max 12 Words:** Section titles (H2) should be concise and descriptive
- **Active Sentences:** Use Subject + Verb + Object structure when possible
- **Concrete Anchor:** Every H2 should contain a **Specific Noun** and a **Concrete Detail** (this can be a number, date, mechanism, or specific qualitative description from the sources)

**Visual & Formatting Toolkit:**
- **Markdown Tables:** Use for comparing "Old vs. New", "Before vs. After", or contrasting perspectives
- **Mermaid Diagrams:** Use `graph TD` for process flows, mechanisms, or relationships
- **Blockquotes:** Use `> Blockquotes` for verbatim quotes from video transcripts
- **Bullet Lists:** Use bullet lists in **every section** to break up prose and improve readability

**Text Styling:**
- **Bold key details:** When sources provide specific numbers or metrics, **bold the noun and the number** together (e.g., "**Net margins fell to 4%**"). Use qualitative descriptions when sources don't provide exact numbers.
