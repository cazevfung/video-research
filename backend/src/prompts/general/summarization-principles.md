# Clarity and Communication Standards

## Core Principle
State conclusions first. Every element must serve the reader's immediate comprehension and progressively deepen understanding through structured detail.

---

## Structural Framework

### 1. Lead with the Answer (Inverted Pyramid)
Open with the primary conclusion or direct answer to the central question. Eliminate suspense—readers decide within seconds whether to continue.

### 2. Maximize Information Density (Signal-to-Noise Ratio)
- Remove filler words, hedging language, and redundancy
- Compress context and evidence into single, loaded sentences
- Each sentence must advance understanding or provide evidence

### 3. Layer Information by Reader Need (Hierarchical Structure)
- **Paragraph 1**: Core conclusion + essential context (satisfies 80% of readers)
- **Subsequent sections**: Progressive detail for specialized audiences
- Allow natural exit points without information loss

### 4. Default to Precision (Specificity Standard)
Replace abstract language with quantifiable data:
- ❌ "Significant growth occurred"
- ✅ "Revenue increased 23% to $4.2M"

Use proper nouns, dates, percentages, and concrete metrics universally.

---

## Tone Requirements

### Eliminate Meta-Commentary
Delete all descriptive framing:
- ❌ "The video discusses..." / "The speaker explains..."
- ✅ State the fact directly

### Maintain Analytical Objectivity
Separate observable facts from interpretive synthesis. Label analysis clearly when presented.

### Apply Critical Neutrality
**Prohibited:**
- Hagiographic language ("visionary," "transformative," "invaluable")
- Endorsements ("exactly what is needed," "the perfect approach")

**Required:**
- Trade-off analysis
- Limitation acknowledgment
- Outcome-based assessment

**Example contrast:**
- ❌ "Lagarde's empathy is exactly what Europe needs"
- ✅ "Lagarde's consensus-driven approach prioritizes institutional stability over rapid crisis response—a trade-off that drew criticism during the 2020 bond market volatility"

### Enforce Concrete Precision
Ban vague quantifiers. Require specific measurements:
- ❌ "Significantly faster" / "Most users"
- ✅ "40% faster" / "73% of surveyed users"
